OPERATIC PLAY — SCENE 6: EVERY TRACK TELLS A STORY
============================================================

DRAMATIS PERSONAE:
  CAPTAIN AARON RHODES  — DJ, curator, keeper of the floor
  SKIPPY THE MAGNIFICENT — AI assistant, flag architect
  SIOUXSIE              — Settled. But noted.
  VARG VIKERNES         — Convicted. Noted thoroughly.
  23,780 TRACKS         — Waiting to be judged

============================================================
ACT I — THE COMMENT

[The show is done. The Captain returns from the floor.
 Summaries sit in the dedup cache. The NML has two empty fields.]

CAPTAIN:
♩ The NML has Comment and Comment 2.
  We should be writing the summary there.
  Reputation flags, lyric flags — everything.
  Let's even overwrite Comments.
  I don't care what's there already. ♩

SKIPPY:
♩ Then nothing is sacred.
  COMMENT gets the summary.
  Always. Unconditionally.
  Sixteen thousand five hundred eighty-two tracks
  now know what they are about. ♩

[write_nml_comments.py is born.
 COMMENT ← summary.
 Every track. Every time. No exceptions.]

============================================================
ACT II — THE SECOND FIELD

CAPTAIN:
♩ And reputation flags.
  And lyric flags.
  All of it. ♩

SKIPPY:
♩ COMMENT2 receives them.
  Theme first — if it exists.
  Then lyric flags, one per danger.
  Then the artist's reputation — tier and name.
  Then the song itself, if the song is the problem.
  Pipe-separated. Compact. Readable at the booth. ♩

[build_comment2() takes four ingredients and returns one string.
 The format:
   theme | ⚑lyric_flag | ⚑rep:tier (Name) | ⚑song:reason
 Any part absent — omitted entirely. No empty pipes.]

============================================================

♩ Sisters of Mercy: clean.
