ACT I — THE MISSING FACES

[The DJ Block Planner glows on screen — cards for every song, dark as space,
 each one a grey anonymous rectangle. No face. No cover. No identity.]

CAPTAIN:
♩ These cards are blind —
  they know not what they look like.
  I want faces on them!
  Album art for every track! ♩

SKIPPY:
♩ Then faces they shall have,
  from Spotify's vast vaults,
  and where Spotify fails us,
  MusicBrainz shall catch the fall.
  And where both fail —
  mutagen reads the file itself. ♩

[A new tool is born: fetch_album_art.py.
 It speaks OAuth2 to Spotify, HTTPS to MusicBrainz,
 and whispers to mutagen when all else is dark.]

SKIPPY:
♩ Each art file lives by its dkey's MD5 —
  artist and title, lowered and tabbed,
  hashed to a hex string,
  a JPEG in state/album_art/.
  The index knows where each face belongs. ♩

[The batch fetcher runs in the background.
 200 tracks... 400... 800...
 Thumbnails bloom in the top-right corner of every card.]

ACT II — THE TRAKTOR PROBLEM

CAPTAIN:
♩ And the library — make Traktor know!
  Let the art live IN the files,
  not just on the web. ♩

SKIPPY:
♩ Ah — there I must confess a dark truth.
  Traktor's own art cache —
  62,000 binary blobs —
  are wrapped in a format unknown.
  Magic bytes 0x084b.
  Custom RGB with delimiters.
  PIL cannot open them.
  No standard shall decode them. ♩

CAPTAIN:
♩ Then bypass the Traktor cache entirely. ♩

SKIPPY:
♩ Instead: embed the art directly
  into every audio file —
  APIC for the MP3s,
  MP4Cover for the M4As,
  FLAC Picture for the lossless.
  Traktor reads the tags directly.
  The booth will know every face. ♩

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

ACT III — THE MYSTERY OF THE UNPLAYED

CAPTAIN:
♩ Something is wrong.
  The played tracking — it fails.
  Songs spin for three minutes
  and are never marked as played.
  Investigate. ♩

SKIPPY:
♩ [examining the code with a magnifying glass]
  Ah. The formula.
  threshold = other_deck_duration + 120 seconds.
  If the other deck holds a four-minute song —
  the threshold becomes THREE HUNDRED SIXTY SECONDS.
  No three-minute track could ever qualify.
  We were requiring the impossible. ♩

CAPTAIN:
♩ Madness. Fix it. ♩

SKIPPY:
♩ The solo-timer approach:
  when one deck's file closes,
  start the clock on the survivor.
  One hundred forty seconds of uncontested play —
  THAT is a played track.
  No duration math. No paradox.
  Just time. ♩

[SOLO_PLAYED_SECS = 140 is written into the code.
 The graveyard of unplayed songs is emptied.
 The setlist fills.]

ACT IV — THE FASHION SHOW

CAPTAIN:
♩ The UI — make it LCARS.
  Star Trek. The Enterprise.
  The computer that Picard trusts. ♩

SKIPPY:
♩ At once — orange and gold and lavender!
  Oswald font, pill-shaped buttons,
  solid colored column headers,
  black as the void of space! ♩

CAPTAIN:
♩ But wait — I need it outdoors too.
  A daylight mode. Warm beige.
  For the outdoor festival gig. ♩

SKIPPY:
♩ Then four themes shall be born:
  NIGHT — the original, dark, red accent.
  DAY — warm beige, ochre, for the sun.
  LCARS — Star Trek gold, pill buttons, Oswald.
  BORG — green on black, Courier New,
         resistance is futile. ♩

[The entire CSS block is torn out and rebuilt.
 CSS variables cascade through every element.
 :root is Night. body.day. body.lcars. body.borg.
 Structural overrides scope the alien aesthetics.]

SKIPPY:
♩ And the button cycles —
  🌙 → ☀ → 🖖 → 👾 → 🌙
  localStorage remembers your allegiance
  between sessions. ♩

CAPTAIN'S WISDOM

  "The threshold was the trap.
   The formula ate itself.
   Always measure from the moment of departure —
   never from the duration of the departed."

  "Traktor's binary art cache is a fortress with no door.
   Don't knock. Build your own house."

  "Four themes are not complexity.
   Four themes are options.
   The DJ who plays outdoors and indoors
   deserves a UI that knows the difference."
