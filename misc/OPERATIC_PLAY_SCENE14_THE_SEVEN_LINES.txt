OPERATIC PLAY — SCENE 14
THE SEVEN LINES

DRAMATIS PERSONAE
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
CAPTAIN AARON RHODES  — Master of the booth, keeper of the cues,
                         patron saint of the karaoke teleprompter
SKIPPY THE MAGNIFICENT — Agentic AI, flask server whisperer,
                          solver of the seven-line problem
THE LIBRARY            — 21,426 souls, each with a story and a BPM
WUMPSCUT               — Ghost in the machine, test track, victim
THE SNOG GANG          — 878 misnamed files, now restored to glory
LRCLIB                 — The oracle of timestamps (cameo)

ACT I — THE WRONG TEXT

[Mac Bilby loads. The karaoke panel appears. The Captain squints.]

CAPTAIN:
♩ That text is not the lyrics —
  that is a summary!
  AI wrote "a haunting blend of
  industrial and misery"
  I want the words, the WORDS,
  the words that humans sing! ♩

SKIPPY:
♩ Forgive me, Captain, I must confess —
  I read from the wrong address.
  KEY_LYRICS holds the synopsis,
  LRCLIB's plain text is what we missed.
  I'll load the lyrics_raw.json file,
  and fix this in a verse, not quite a mile. ♩

[Skippy redirects to PLAIN_CACHE. The text transforms.
"The Great Commandment" appears. The Captain exhales.]

ACT II — THE FROZEN SCROLL

CAPTAIN:
♩ The text is there! The words are right!
  But nothing moves — it's frozen tight.
  Six minutes pass, no scroll, no tick,
  this karaoke wall is making me sick. ♩

SKIPPY:
♩ Ah — the history file is five days old.
  HistoryWatcher sees no tale to be told.
  No crossfade logged means no position flows,
  the SSE pipe runs silent — nobody knows.
  I shall add a wall-clock timer, 200ms,
  ticking from the moment of detection — no less! ♩

[A setInterval is born. The lines begin to move.
The Captain watches Wolfsheim scroll past his eyes.]

CAPTAIN:
♩ The karaoke is sort of working!
  The words are not synced with the beat —
  but it progresseth! It marcheth onward!
  This is already a feat! ♩

ACT III — THE CUE POINT REVELATION

CAPTAIN:
♩ But the text starts from the intro,
  scrolling through the silent part —
  I do not need to read blank emptiness
  before the music starts.
  I think we set some cue points,
  number three and number six —
  Vocal In and Vocal Out, old friends —
  can those cues fix this? ♩

SKIPPY:
♩ HOTCUE two is Vocal In!
  HOTCUE five is Vocal Out!
  We parse them both from NML at load —
  the vocal window: mapped throughout!
  Distribute the lines across that span,
  before: blank silence; after: done.
  The teleprompter sleeps until the singer sings —
  it wakes precisely, line by line it rings! ♩

[The math is written. estimateLines() receives
vocalInMs, vocalOutMs. The intro scrolls no more.]

ACT IV — THE EXPANSION

CAPTAIN:
♩ Three lines! Give me five!
  Five lines! Give me seven!
  I want a waterfall of lyrics
  cascading straight to heaven! ♩

SKIPPY:
♩ 20 / 35 / 65 —
  the past recedes in fading grey.
  100 — the present burns full bright.
  65 / 35 / 20 — the future waits in dimming light. ♩

CAPTAIN:
♩ It's brilliant.
  It's BRILLIANT.
  Seven lines of gorgeous fade —
  the finest teleprompter
  this dark DJ booth has made! ♩

[Standing ovation. Snog plays in the background.
Then — ERROR: File cannot be played.]

ACT V — THE SNOG AFFAIR

CAPTAIN:
♩ Some tracks refuse to load!
  Traktor says the file is gone!
  What happened to my Snog,
  my Wumpscut, all along? ♩

SKIPPY:
♩ The _2 files — remember Stage Two?
  When collisions led to foo_2.mp3 anew?
  The originals were moved away,
  the _2 remained — lost, astray.
  878 orphans, renamed back to right,
  185 true duplicates deleted from sight.
  Nine NML entries pointed to the void —
  those too: removed and then destroyed. ♩

CAPTAIN:
♩ I prefer to rename before I delete —
  check originals first, make sure they're complete! ♩

SKIPPY:
♩ The originals were verified present, Captain —
  no unique content passed from this world.
  But your preference is logged, and the doctrine updated:
  rename first; then let deletion be unfurled. ♩

ACT VI — THE WORD THAT BOLDS

[The LRC backfill runs. 300 tracks receive timestamps.
Wolfsheim. Lebanon Hanover. The 69 Eyes.]

CAPTAIN:
♩ If word sync works, then bold the word —
  just bold it, move it, nothing blurred.
  No spans, no classes, just a B —
  sweep left to right so I can see. ♩

SKIPPY:
♩ In LRC mode only — line timestamps known —
  I interpolate: how far have we gone?
  Split the line, find the word at index wi,
  wrap it in bold and let the others stand by.
  Estimated mode stays plain and still —
  rough timing cannot earn this thrill. ♩

[The function is written. Bilby restarts.
Delerium plays. Line by line. Word by word.
The bold sweeps left to right across the current line
like a finger on a page in the dark.]

ACT VII — THE OVERNIGHT COMMISSION

CAPTAIN:
♩ Check it all in. The night is long.
  DJ Bilby on the PC plays his own song.
  Begin the backfill — 23,547 remain —
  let LRCLIB do its work while I refrain. ♩

SKIPPY:
♩ Git commit. Push. The code is clean.
  The overnight queue: the largest ever seen.
  By morning, Captain, half your library sings
  in line-accurate time, with words that bling.
  Sleep well. The teleprompter holds the fort —
  seven lines of light in the dark. ♩

[Exeunt. The terminal blinks: requests/second: 1.
23,547 → 23,546 → 23,545...]
