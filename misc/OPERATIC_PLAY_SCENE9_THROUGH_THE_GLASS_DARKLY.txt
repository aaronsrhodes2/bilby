ACT I — THE BLACK CANVAS

[Morning. The DJ Block Planner is open. Four themes cycle —
 Night, Day, LCARS, Borg. The Captain squints at the screen
 through glasses that are not yet on his face.]

CAPTAIN:
♩ A fifth theme I desire —
  one called "passthrough."
  #000000 black as the void between stars,
  so the real world bleeds through
  when I wear the glass. ♩

SKIPPY:
♩ A mode, not a theme —
  I shall hide the track count,
  the deck pills, the waiting text,
  the reset button, the OSC lamp,
  the Show Setup, the tooltip,
  the activity bar —
  all retired into the black. ♩

[The fifth theme is born. 🕶 icon added.
 The cycle expands: Night → Day → LCARS → Borg → Passthrough.
 Pure black reigns.]

CAPTAIN:
♩ And number each candidate,
  so I may call them by number —
  "select three" shall make it so. ♩

SKIPPY:
♩ Keyword grammar, seven verbs strong:
  swap decks, save me, surprise me,
  open setlist, open show,
  select N, select A or B.
  Enter in the search box fires the match.
  No match? Falls through to search. ♩

ACT II — THE FOUR ZONES

[The Captain has been reading about cognition.]

CAPTAIN:
♩ Hear me on spatial law.
  TOP is the world — what comes in from outside.
  BOTTOM is the self — my words, my choices.
  CORNERS are for glance — things I dismiss often.
  SIDES are for manipulation — minimise these,
    for I shall not touch the UI
    unless I must. ♩

SKIPPY:
♩ Flex-column body with order directives —
  hdr order one,
  deck-strip order two,
  cols flex:1 filling the middle,
  selected-strip order four,
  search wrap order five,
  action buttons order six.
  Zones become law. ♩

[A first attempt: strips top and bottom, 2-column middle.
 The Captain frowns through his glasses.]

CAPTAIN:
♩ No — big card for the Selected Song,
  filling the space between decks and title.
  With all the flags, all the detail.
  Decks side-by-side at the bottom of column one,
  above the action buttons.
  Push them down. ♩

SKIPPY:
♩ Col 1 becomes three strata:
  label at the head,
  anchor card flex:1,
  deck-cards margin-top:auto —
  gravity pulls the decks
  to the very bottom of the column. ♩

ACT III — THE IMPOSSIBLE RECTANGLE

CAPTAIN:
♩ Nineteen-twenty by twelve-hundred.
  No scrollbars, no wrapped text,
  no ellipsis cutoff.
  Always legible.
  Dynamic.
  Make it so. ♩

SKIPPY:
♩ A three-pass fit.
  First: horizontal —
  each single line measured,
  font shrunk by half-pixels
  until scrollWidth fits clientWidth.
  Floor: ten pixels.
  Second: vertical —
  if a column body overflows,
  every card font steps down together.
  Third: size-justified —
  for paragraphs,
  I binary-search the largest size
  where the rendered rectangle
  fills the container exactly.
  No wrapping. No cutoff.
  Text meets edge on all four sides. ♩

[Short summaries grow large and readable.
 Long summaries shrink to fit tight.
 Either way, the rectangle is flush.]

ACT IV — VOICE AND CURSOR

CAPTAIN:
♩ When this window is active,
  the cursor shall go to the search prompt.
  And the search prompt shall listen —
  continuously, from the microphone. ♩

SKIPPY:
♩ Auto-focus watcher:
  on focus, on visibilitychange-visible,
  on any click that isn't on a card or button —
  the input field takes the cursor.
  No hand required to begin. ♩

♩ And a microphone button: 🎤
  Web Speech API, continuous mode.
  Interim transcripts live in the box.
  Final transcripts fire the parser —
  command or search, routed in silence.
  Red pulsing shadow while listening.
  Chrome's silence timeout defeats not us:
  onend handler restarts the stream. ♩

ACT V — THE TAG OF NO WORDS

CAPTAIN:
♩ Tag the instrumentals.
  Let them glow violet. ♩

SKIPPY:
♩ One predicate unites all signals —
  title patterns of "instrumental" and "inst.",
  "no vocals", "karaoke",
  theme equals instrumental from STT,
  summary prefix "Instrumental — ".
  ♬ INSTR pill
  and a violet left-border.
  Seventy tracks light up today;
  the rest will kindle
  when the PC runs its Whisper. ♩

ACT VI — THE DISPATCH

CAPTAIN:
♩ Send the PC three jobs.
  Instrumentals, lyrics, missing cues.
  And since you cannot trigger yourself,
  build the trigger too. ♩

SKIPPY:
♩ A dispatch file in git —
  DISPATCH_PC_RUN_ALL, dated today.
  Baseline table at the top,
  Job Zero for Bootstrap,
  Jobs One and Two for the work,
  and a Run-This prompt at the bottom
  the PC's Claude can paste into action. ♩

♩ And for the trigger —
  pc_dispatch_watcher.py,
  a polling sentinel:
  every sixty seconds it fetches,
  pulls only fast-forward,
  verifies the remote URL,
  scans tools/ for unprocessed dispatches,
  extracts their Run-This block,
  and invokes the Claude CLI
  with dangerously-skip-permissions.
  Each dispatch runs exactly once,
  state remembered in JSON.
  Install as a Windows Scheduled Task.
  Kill switch: STOP_WATCHER file in git.
  One paste and the chain is closed —
  every future dispatch,
  autonomous. ♩

ACT VII — THE GREATER ARC

[Late in the session. The Captain sets aside the DJ app
 and speaks of something larger.]

CAPTAIN:
♩ The phone stays in the pocket.
  Two USB-C ports —
  one for the glasses' display,
  one for the battery.
  The phone sleeps, the glasses see.
  The OS is one app —
  many views, one input,
  read-only output,
  never overlapping,
  an inventory of verbs
  the user may ask at any time. ♩

SKIPPY:
♩ What we built today
  is the per-view engine.
  What remains is the host —
  a dispatcher that fans input
  out to every registered view,
  first refusal to the active one,
  fallthrough ranked by confidence,
  the inventory always true
  to what the app can actually do.
  The DJ is one tape-out;
  the host is the paradigm. ♩

CAPTAIN'S WISDOM

  "The hardest problem in voice is: what verbs exist?
   The solution is the inventory —
   a living list of every verb every view knows right now.
   Discoverability is the compass."

  "Corners are for glance. Bottom is self. Top is the world.
   Sides are manipulation. Minimise the sides."

  "The rectangle is fixed. The text fills it exactly.
   Never wrap. Never cut. Never scroll.
   Shrink the font until the words meet the edges."

  "Read-only by default. Input-driven.
   One tape, many state machines."

 The Captain still has not put on the glasses.
 The dispatch sits in git, unopened.
 The watcher, not yet installed, dreams of its future loop.
 Somewhere a phone charges, two ports, both occupied.
 The music is coming.]
