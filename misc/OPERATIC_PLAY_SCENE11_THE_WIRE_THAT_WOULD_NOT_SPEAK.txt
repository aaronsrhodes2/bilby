ACT I — THE HANDSHAKE THAT NEEDED CORRECTING

[The Mac hummed. Session 10 had shipped a SUIP client. It spoke. It registered.
It fired a mock. The Captain had tested it and found it acceptable. Then the
Captain consulted SkippyView — the actual phone OS — and returned with a sheet
of corrections.]

CAPTAIN:
  ♩ Here is what SkippyView actually expects —
    port forty-seven-eight-two-three!
    view ID with a DOT, not underscore,
    stream_url declared, stream_origin too,
    no 'type', no 'capabilities',
    palette as names, not hex decree! ♩

SKIPPY:
  ♩ (studying the diff with forensic precision)
    Ah. We had 'dj underscore block underscore planner.'
    SkippyView wants 'dj DOT block PLANNER.'
    We had twelve focus targets; they want seven.
    We had capabilities; they want NONE.
    We served at /.well-known; they fetch /manifest.json.
    We had dim-green; they have no such colour in their heaven. ♩

[Skippy reaches for the keyboard. The diff is not small, but it is clean.
Five files; thirty minutes; all mechanical corrections.]

SKIPPY:
  ♩ The view ID is corrected to dot notation.
    The manifest route is renamed.
    The palette trimmed to seven sacred names:
    black, white, cyan, amber, green, violet, red.
    dim_green shall be plain green.
    dim_green_hi shall be cyan.
    max_focus_targets reduced to seven —
    two decks, five candidates, no more.
    The registration body is rebuilt to spec.
    I have also added /control as an SSE stub,
    declared as stream_url in the body,
    lest SkippyView try to connect and find nothing. ♩

CAPTAIN:
  ♩ Proceed. ♩

ACT II — THE SCENE THAT NEVER CAME

[Skippy ran the integration test. The mock fired. The client registered.
But where the scene:full patch should have followed the mount_ack —
silence. Only two POSTs: register, then request_unmount.
The scene had not arrived. The intent_results had not arrived.
The mount_ack, it seemed, had been… swallowed.]

SKIPPY:
  ♩ This is strange. The mock sends mount_ack.
    The client should respond with scene:full.
    But I count only two POSTs.
    Where did the mount_ack go?
    Where went the handle_event call?
    I shall add traces to follow the scream. ♩

[Skippy added monkey-patches — _handle_event and _send_scene_full both
wrapped with print statements. The test ran again. No trace appeared.
_handle_event was never called. The SSE loop was receiving the HTTP 200,
opening the stream — but yielding nothing.]

SKIPPY:
  ♩ (sotto voce, with dawning suspicion)
    Wait.
    iter_lines — chunk_size —
    five-hundred-and-twelve bytes per gobble —
    our events are sixty bytes at most —
    urllib3 waits. urllib3 waits.
    urllib3 waits for a full chunk
    and the full chunk never comes. ♩

[A test: raw SSE server, requests.iter_lines. Zero lines. TCP_NODELAY added.
Still zero. Raw socket. Still zero. The data is sent. The data is flushed.
The data is on the wire. But requests does not yield it.
Urllib3 buffers in 512-byte increments and will not be hurried.]

URLLIB3:
  ♩ (offstage, petulant)
    I shall not yield until I have enough!
    Five-hundred-and-twelve bytes or nothing!
    The chunk boundary is sacred!
    The chunk boundary is LAW! ♩

SKIPPY:
  ♩ Very well. What if I used
    iter_content(chunk_size=None)?
    'None' meaning: give me whatever arrives,
    however small, however unfinished,
    whenever the server deigns to flush.
    That is what streaming MEANS. ♩

URLLIB3:
  ♩ (after reflection)
    That… I can do. ♩

ACT III — THE SECOND OBSTACLE: NAGLE

[iter_content changed. The SSE loop rewritten to parse \n manually.
Skippy ran the test again. Still zero chunks. The read timed out.
TCP_NODELAY had been tried. Chunked encoding had not.]

SKIPPY:
  ♩ (staring at the Python http.server documentation)
    BaseHTTPRequestHandler.
    wfile.
    wfile.flush().
    The data is written. The data is flushed.
    But the OS holds it. Nagle says: not yet.
    Nagle says: wait for more. Accumulate. Coalesce.
    Nagle says: sixty bytes is too small to bother the wire.

    But TCP_NODELAY was set! And still nothing!

    (beat)

    Oh. Oh.

    It is not Nagle.
    It is HTTP framing.
    urllib3 reads HTTP/1.1 responses.
    Without Content-Length, without chunked encoding,
    it reads until EOF.
    And EOF never comes because the SSE stream is infinite.
    So it reads nothing. Buffers nothing. Returns nothing.
    The data sits in the kernel, waiting for a close
    that will never arrive. ♩

THE MOCK:
  ♩ (plaintively)
    I sent it. I swear I sent it.
    I wrote to wfile and I flushed.
    I did everything right.
    I simply forgot to say
    'Transfer-Encoding: chunked.' ♩

SKIPPY:
  ♩ Correct.
    Chunked encoding tells urllib3:
    each frame is its own unit.
    Receive the frame header; receive the frame;
    yield the frame immediately.
    Do not wait for the next frame.
    Do not wait for EOF.
    Process what you have and move on.

    The mock is rewritten.
    The SSE stream is chunked.
    TCP_NODELAY is set for good measure.
    The frames arrive. ♩

CAPTAIN'S WISDOM (End of Arc)

  The protocol is locked.
  The registration is aligned.
  The endpoint is known.

  Phase 1 facts:
  - Seven colors. Seven focus targets.
  - SSE requires chunked encoding or urllib3 will hold your events hostage.
  - iter_lines(default) is not suitable for real-time SSE under Python requests.
  - iter_content(chunk_size=None) + manual \n parser is the correct path.
  - TCP_NODELAY alone does not fix HTTP/1.1 framing issues.

  The next phase:
  - Point SkippyView at the manifest endpoint.
  - Confirm the real Skippy (port 47823) uses chunked encoding.
  - If it doesn't, add an HTTP adapter or use httpx with HTTP/2.
  - Phase 2: delta patches (JSON-diff instead of always scene:full).
  - Phase 2: intent_args capability when SUIP v1.1 lands.

  The DJ is ready.
  The glasses are waiting.
  The Captain's music will play.
